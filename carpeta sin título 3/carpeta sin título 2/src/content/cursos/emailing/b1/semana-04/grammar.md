# Grammar (B1) · Semana 04

> Objetivo: pedir clarificación y confirmar detalles sin sonar crítico, para evitar malentendidos.

## 1) Clarificar (sin sonar agresivo)

| Intención | Patrón (EN) | Ejemplo (EN) |
|---|---|---|
| Clarificar suavemente | Just to clarify, … | Just to clarify, the deadline is Friday. |
| Pedir confirmación | Could you confirm … ? | Could you confirm the final version number? |
| Preguntar “qué significa” | When you say X, do you mean Y? | When you say “final”, do you mean approved? |

> Tip: “Just to clarify” reduce tensión. No implica culpa.

---

## 2) Confirmar detalles (resumen en 1 línea)

| Uso | Patrón (EN) | Ejemplo (EN) |
|---|---|---|
| Resumen corto | So, we are aligned on … | So, we are aligned on the scope and timeline. |
| Confirmar checklist | Just to confirm: A, B, C. | Just to confirm: draft by Tue, review by Thu, final on Fri. |
| Cierre con next step | If that’s correct, I’ll proceed. | If that’s correct, I’ll proceed and share the final file. |

---

## 3) Evitar malentendidos típicos

- Could you confirm which version you’re referring to?
- Could you confirm the deadline (Friday) is still correct?
- If I’m理解 wrong / If I’m mistaken… (mejor B1: If I’m mistaken, please let me know.)

> Regla rápida: 1) clarifica 2) confirma 3) next step.
