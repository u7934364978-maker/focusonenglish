# Grammar (B1) · Semana 01

> Objetivo: escribir emails claros y neutros usando peticiones educadas + deadlines suaves.

## 1) Peticiones educadas (patrones base)

| Intención | Patrón (EN) | Ejemplo (EN) |
|---|---|---|
| Pedir acción | Could you please + VERB (base)? | Could you please review the draft? |
| Pedir con fecha | Could you please + VERB … by + day/date? | Could you please approve it by Friday? |
| Deadline suave | If possible, could you … by + day/date? | If possible, could you approve it by Friday? |
| Extra suavidad | By + day would be great (if possible). | By Thursday would be great if possible. |

> Regla rápida: después de **please** va el **verbo en base** (review / approve / share), sin **to**.

**✅ Correcto**
- Could you please review it?
- Could you please approve it?

**❌ Incorrecto**
- Could you please *to* review it?
- Could you please *reviewing* it?

---

## 2) Propósito claro (la primera frase)
| Función | Ejemplo (EN) |
|---|---|
| Propósito directo | I’m writing to ask for your approval on the attached draft. |
| Registro un poco más formal | I’m writing to request your approval on the attached draft. |

> Si el propósito está claro en la primera frase, el email se entiende en 5 segundos.

---

## 3) Adjuntos (frases cortas y limpias)
- Attached is the updated draft for your review.
- Please find the updated draft attached.
