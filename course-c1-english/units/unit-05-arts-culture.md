### L3 — Grammar: Advanced Narrative Tenses

**Forms:**

| Tense | Use in narrative | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Past simple | Main events (foreground) | *She opened the door and saw him standing there.* |
| Past continuous | Background / interrupted action | *The rain was falling heavily when the phone rang.* |
| Past perfect simple | Earlier past event | *By the time she arrived, he had already left.* |
| Past perfect continuous | Earlier ongoing action / cause | *She had been waiting for two hours before he appeared.* |
| Would + infinitive | Habitual past action | *Every evening, he would sit by the window and write.* |

**Complex sequences:**
- *Having been working on the novel for years, she finally submitted the manuscript.*
- *She realised she had been deceived all along — the clues had been there from the beginning.*

**Exercises:**
1. Choose the correct narrative tense in 10 sentences
2. Write a 120-word paragraph of narration using all 5 narrative tenses
3. Identify and explain the tense choice in 6 literary sentences

### L3 — Grammar: Complex Adjectival Phrases

**Forms:**

| Type | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Multi-word premodifier | adj + adj + noun | *a bold, geometrically precise composition* |
| Noun adjunct | noun + noun | *a steel-and-glass structure* |

| Postmodifier (relative clause) | noun + relative clause | *a painting that defies conventional categorisation* |
| Stacked postmodifiers | noun + multiple phrases | *an artist whose work, characterised by bold contrasts, challenged the prevailing aesthetic* |

### L3 — Grammar: Structures of Degree and Result

**Forms:**

| Structure | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *so + adj + that* | *so + adj/adv + that + clause* | *The performance was so moving that many in the audience wept.* |
| *such + noun phrase + that* | *such + (a/an) + adj + noun + that* | *It was such a powerful piece that it silenced the hall.* |
| *too + adj + to* | *too + adj/adv + to-infinitive* | *The composition was too complex to be performed live.* |
| *adj + enough + to* | *adj + enough + to-infinitive* | *She was talented enough to secure a recording contract at 16.* |
| *enough + noun + to* | *enough + noun + to-infinitive* | *There was enough evidence to suggest a major influence.* |

**Common errors to avoid:**
- ✗ *so talented that she could to perform* → ✓ *could perform*
- ✗ *too difficult for perform* → ✓ *too difficult to perform*
- ✗ *enough talented* → ✓ *talented enough*

**Exercises:**
1. Combine 8 sentence pairs using *so/such…that*
2. Rewrite 6 sentences using *too…to* or *enough…to*
3. Write 4 original sentences about music using different degree structures

### L3 — Grammar: Advanced Inversion

**Forms:**

| Adverbial | Inverted structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *Not until* | *Not until + clause + auxiliary + subject* | *Not until the final scene does the truth become clear.* |

### L3 — Grammar: Nominalisation

**Purpose:** Converting verbs and adjectives into nouns to achieve formal, dense academic register.

**Common patterns:**

| Verb/Adjective | Nominalised form | Suffix |
|---|---|---|
| *decide* | *decision* | -ion |
| *develop* | *development* | -ment |
| *preserve* | *preservation* | -ation |
| *significant* | *significance* | -ance |
| *achieve* | *achievement* | -ment |
| *deprive* | *deprivation* | -ation |
| *collaborate* | *collaboration* | -ation |
| *fragile* | *fragility* | -ity |

**Transformation:**
- Informal: *When governments failed to act, communities suffered.*
- Formal: *Government failure to act resulted in community suffering.*
- Academic: *The failure of governments to act had significant consequences for affected communities.*

**Exercises:**
1. Nominalise 10 verbs/adjectives and write each in an original sentence
2. Transform 6 informal sentences into formal register using nominalisation
3. Identify all nominalisations in an academic paragraph and comment on their effect
