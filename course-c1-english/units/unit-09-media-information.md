### L3 — Grammar: Comparing and Contrasting Structures

**Forms:**

| Function | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Comparison** | *compared to/with* | *Compared to print, digital news reaches audiences instantly.* |
| **Contrast** | *in contrast to/with, unlike* | *Unlike traditional outlets, social media platforms have no editorial gatekeeping.* |
| **Negated similarity** | *far from being, rather than* | *Far from being dead, print journalism has found a niche audience.* |
| **Partial similarity** | *just as…so too, like* | *Just as radio adapted to television, print is adapting to digital.* |
| **Degree of difference** | *considerably/significantly/far + more/less* | *Digital media is considerably more accessible than print.* |
| **Similarity** | *similarly, likewise, in the same way* | *Similarly, broadcasters have had to rethink their business models.* |

**Exercises:**

### L3 — Grammar: Distancing Language

**Purpose:** To report claims without endorsing them, or to cast doubt on their accuracy.

**Forms:**

| Structure | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *allegedly* | Reported but unverified (formal/legal) | *The platform allegedly knew about the manipulation.* |
| *supposedly* | Generally believed but questionable | *The supposedly independent fact-checkers had links to the platform.* |
| *so-called* | Challenges the named label | *The so-called experts quoted had no relevant qualifications.* |
| *what is described as* | Reports a description without endorsing it | *What is described as a grassroots movement appears to be centrally coordinated.* |
| *purportedly* | Claims to be (but may not be) | *The document, purportedly leaked from within the company…* |
| *it is claimed/alleged that* | Passive distancing | *It is alleged that the data was manipulated before publication.* |
| *according to + source* | Attributed, not endorsed | *According to the company's own figures, engagement fell by 40%.* |

**Exercises:**
1. Rewrite 8 sentences to add appropriate distancing language
2. Identify the distancing device and its purpose in 6 sentences
3. Write a 100-word media analysis paragraph using 4 different distancing structures

### L3 — Grammar: Impersonal Constructions

**Forms:**

| Structure | Example |
|---|---|
| *One tends to + infinitive* | *One tends to underestimate the influence of algorithmic curation.* |
| *One is inclined to* | *One is inclined to assume that viral content reflects public opinion.* |
| *People are known to* | *People are known to share content without reading beyond the headline.* |
| *It is widely believed/thought* | *It is widely believed that social media increases political polarisation.* |
| *It has been observed that* | *It has been observed that echo chambers reinforce existing beliefs.* |
| *It would be naive to* | *It would be naive to assume platforms self-regulate effectively.* |
| *There is a tendency to* | *There is a growing tendency to conflate popularity with credibility.* |

**Use notes:**
- *One* is formal and academic; avoid in informal contexts
- These structures allow generalisation without claiming personal experience
- Common in academic essays, journalism and formal speeches

### L3 — Grammar: Advanced Cleft Sentences

**Forms:**

| Type | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ***What*-cleft (subject focus)** | *What + clause + is/was + focused element* | *What concerns civil libertarians most is the vagueness of the legislation.* |
| ***What*-cleft (verb focus)** | *What + subject + did + was + infinitive* | *What the court did was set a dangerous precedent.* |
| ***It*-cleft (time)** | *It was + time expression + that* | *It was only in the 20th century that free speech became a legal right.* |
| **Reason cleft** | *The reason (why)… is that…* | *The reason why hate speech laws are controversial is that definitions vary.* |
| **Way cleft** | *The way (in which)… is…* | *The way in which platforms moderate content is inconsistent.* |
| **Reverse cleft** | *X is what/who…* | *Consistency is what the law requires above all else.* |

**Exercises:**
1. Rewrite 8 sentences as advanced cleft sentences using different types
2. Identify the focused element in 6 cleft sentences and explain why it is foregrounded
3. Write a 100-word legal analysis paragraph using 3 different cleft types
