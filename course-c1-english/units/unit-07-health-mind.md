### L3 — Grammar: Nominal Clauses

**Purpose:** Nominal clauses function as nouns — as subject, object or complement of a sentence.

**Forms:**

| Function | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Subject** | *That + clause + main verb* | *That mental illness remains stigmatised is deeply concerning.* |
| **Subject (formal)** | *What + clause + is/was* | *What concerns experts most is the rise in youth anxiety.* |
| **Object** | *verb + that + clause* | *Research suggests that early intervention is highly effective.* |
| **Complement** | *be + that-clause* | *The truth is that many people never seek help.* |
| **Extraposed subject** | *It + be + adj + that + clause* | *It is essential that mental health services be adequately funded.* |

**Use notes:**
- In formal register, *that* is often retained (not omitted as in informal speech)
- *What*-nominal clauses are particularly common in academic and formal writing

### L3 — Grammar: Advanced Reporting Verbs

**Patterns:**

| Pattern | Verbs | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *verb + that* | *allege, claim, contend, maintain, argue, concede, acknowledge, insist* | *She contended that the current law was inadequate.* |
| *verb + object + that* | *convince, remind, assure, inform, notify, warn* | *The doctor assured the committee that the procedure was safe.* |
| *verb + -ing* | *deny, admit, justify, mention, recommend, suggest* | *He denied having withheld information from patients.* |
| *verb + object + of* | *accuse, convince, remind, assure* | *Critics accused the government of failing to act.* |
| *verb + to-infinitive* | *agree, offer, refuse, threaten, claim* | *The hospital refused to comment on the case.* |
| *verb + object + to-infinitive* | *urge, encourage, advise, persuade, warn* | *Ethicists urged legislators to consider the full implications.* |

**Nuance between verbs:**
- *claim* (speaker believes it, others may not) vs *maintain* (sustained, firm position)
- *acknowledge* (admit a fact that may be inconvenient) vs *concede* (give ground in an argument)
- *allege* (unproven, often legal context) vs *assert* (stated firmly as true)

**Exercises:**
1. Choose the most appropriate reporting verb in 10 sentences
2. Rewrite 6 direct quotes using appropriate reporting verbs in correct patterns
3. Identify and classify 6 reporting verbs in a formal text

### L3 — Grammar: Expressing Concession

**Forms:**

| Type | Structures | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Subordinating conjunctions** | *although, even though, while, whereas* | *Although the diet is popular, there is little clinical evidence for it.* |
| **Prepositional phrases** | *despite, in spite of* | *Despite widespread belief in its benefits, the science is inconclusive.* |
| **Adverbial connectors** | *nevertheless, nonetheless, however, yet* | *The study was small; nevertheless, the findings were significant.* |
| **Adverb** | *still* | *The evidence is limited; we still cannot rule out a benefit.* |
| **Fronted concession** | *Much as… / However… + adj + subject + verb* | *Much as I appreciate the effort, the methodology is flawed.* |
| **Concessive relative** | *which, though true, / who, despite being* | *The results, though promising, need replication.* |

**Use notes:**
- *Although/even though* + full clause · *Despite/in spite of* + noun phrase or *-ing*
- *Nevertheless/nonetheless* connect two sentences (not two clauses)
- *Yet* at the start of a sentence is formal and literary

**Exercises:**
1. Choose the correct concession structure in 10 sentences
2. Combine 8 sentence pairs using an appropriate concession structure
3. Rewrite 4 sentences replacing informal *but* with formal concession structures

### L3 — Grammar: Passive with Reporting Verbs

**Forms:**

| Structure | Example |
|---|---|
| *It is + reported/claimed/believed/said + that* | *It is widely believed that universal healthcare leads to better outcomes.* |
| *It has been + suggested/argued/demonstrated + that* | *It has been demonstrated that preventive care reduces long-term costs.* |
| *Subject + is/are + said/believed/reported + to* | *The system is said to be under unprecedented financial pressure.* |
| *Subject + is/are + thought/known/expected + to* | *Healthcare costs are expected to double by 2050.* |
| *Subject + is/are + alleged/claimed + to have + pp* | *The government is alleged to have mismanaged the health budget.* |

**Use notes:**
- These structures allow the writer to **report claims without endorsing them**
- They are essential for academic writing where attribution and objectivity are required
- The choice between impersonal (*it is said*) and personal (*he is said*) depends on context

**Exercises:**

2. Choose the best passive reporting structure in 6 formal sentences
