### L3 — Grammar: Advanced Obligation Structures

**Forms:**

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *be obliged/compelled to* | External, formal obligation | *States are obliged to protect the rights of all citizens.* |
| *be required to* | Rule or law demands it | *All signatories are required to submit annual reports.* |
| *be duty-bound to* | Moral or professional obligation | *The international community is duty-bound to respond.* |
| *be under an obligation to* | Formal/legal duty | *Governments are under an obligation to uphold international law.* |
| *insist on + -ing* | Demand firmly | *Human rights organisations insist on full accountability.* |
| *it is incumbent on + obj + to* | It is the responsibility of | *It is incumbent on member states to take action.* |
| *it behoves + obj + to* | Formal: it is right/proper for | *It behoves the international community to respond decisively.* |

**Exercises:**
1. Rewrite 8 sentences using a more formal obligation structure
2. Rank 5 obligation structures from weakest to strongest
3. Write a formal 80-word paragraph on state responsibility using 4 different obligation structures

### L3 — Grammar: Fronting and Topicalisation

**Purpose:** Moving elements to the front of a sentence for focus, contrast or emphasis.

**Forms:**

| Type | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Adverbial fronting** | Time/place/manner adverbial at start | *In times of crisis, communities must come together.* |
| **Object fronting** | Object placed before subject (formal) | *This suffering, no one should have to endure.* |
| **Complement fronting** | Adjective/complement at start | *Remarkable is the resilience these families have shown.* |
| **Topicalisation with *as for*** | *As for + topic + clause* | *As for the refugees' future, it remains deeply uncertain.* |
| **Topicalisation with *what*** | *What + topic + is/needs…* | *What we need is a comprehensive, humane policy.* |
| **Cleft for topic focus** | *It is + X + that…* | *It is the most vulnerable who bear the greatest burden.* |

**Exercises:**
1. Rewrite 8 sentences using fronting or topicalisation for emphasis
2. Identify the purpose of fronting in 6 formal sentences
3. Write a 100-word opinion paragraph on migration using 3 different fronting techniques

---

### L3 — Grammar: Expressing Contrast

**Forms:**

| Type | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Subordinating** | *whereas / while* | *Whereas the richest 1% saw their wealth double, the poorest lost ground.* |
| **Sentence connector** | *by contrast / conversely / on the other hand* | *The US has high per-capita income. By contrast, wealth distribution is extremely unequal.* |
| **Prepositional** | *unlike / in contrast to* | *Unlike Nordic countries, the UK has seen a sharp rise in child poverty.* |
| **Concessive** | *despite / in spite of / nevertheless* | *Despite economic growth, inequality has widened.* |
| **Fronted** | *Whereas clause + main clause* | *Whereas access to education has improved, outcomes remain deeply unequal.* |

**Exercises:**
1. Combine 8 sentence pairs using a contrast structure
2. Replace informal *but* with formal contrast structures in 6 sentences
3. Write a 100-word paragraph contrasting two countries' approaches to poverty reduction

### L3 — Grammar: Advanced Adverbial Clauses

**Forms:**

| Type | Conjunctions | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | *so that, in order that, lest* | *Nations cooperate so that conflicts can be avoided.* |
| **Result** | *such…that, so…that* | *Tensions had risen to such an extent that diplomacy broke down.* |
| **Concessive** | *even if, even though, much as, however + adj* | *Even if agreement is reached, implementation will be difficult.* |
| **Condition** | *provided that, on condition that, unless, were + subject* | *Were tensions to escalate, the consequences would be severe.* |
| **Reason** | *seeing that, in that, given that* | *Given that both sides have nuclear capabilities, restraint is essential.* |
| **Manner** | *as if, as though, in the way that* | *The agreement collapsed as though it had never been signed.* |

**Exercises:**
1. Complete 8 sentences with the correct adverbial conjunction
2. Identify the type and function of adverbial clauses in 6 sentences

---

### L3 — Grammar: Expressing Speculation and Possibility

**Forms:**

| Structure | Degree of certainty | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *might well* | Quite possible | *This approach might well succeed where others have failed.* |
| *could conceivably* | Possible but not obvious | *A negotiated settlement could conceivably be reached within months.* |
| *there is every chance that* | Optimistic possibility | *There is every chance that dialogue will resume.* |
| *it is feasible that* | Technically possible | *It is feasible that a two-state solution could still be achieved.* |
| *it is not inconceivable that* | Cautious: possible despite doubts | *It is not inconceivable that the ceasefire will hold.* |
| *it remains to be seen whether* | Uncertain outcome | *It remains to be seen whether the agreement will be honoured.* |
| *one cannot rule out* | Cannot be excluded | *One cannot rule out the possibility of further escalation.* |

**Exercises:**
1. Replace *maybe* or *perhaps* with more sophisticated speculation structures in 8 sentences
2. Rank 5 speculation structures from least to most confident
3. Write a 100-word speculative paragraph on a geopolitical scenario using 4 different structures

---
