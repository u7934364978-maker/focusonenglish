# Vocabulary Framework — C1 English Course

> **CEFR Level:** C1 Advanced  
> **Total vocabulary lessons:** 60 (L4 of every unit)  
> **Target vocabulary range:** ~3,500–4,000 active items across the course  
> **Academic Word List (AWL):** Integrated across all modules, with increasing density from Module 5 onwards

---

## Vocabulary Progression Principles

### B2 → C1 Shift
| B2 Vocabulary Competence | C1 Target |
|---|---|
| Knows common collocations | Knows precise collocations and restrictions |
| Uses phrasal verbs in context | Uses sophisticated phrasal verbs and fixed phrases |
| Basic word formation | Advanced derivational morphology and conversion |
| Familiar with some idioms | Uses idioms and fixed expressions naturally |
| General academic words | AWL sublists 1–10 integrated in context |

### Four-Strand Approach (per unit)
1. **Meaning in context** — encountering new words in reading/listening texts
2. **Form and collocations** — studying word forms and typical partners
3. **Production** — using words in writing and speaking tasks
4. **Review and recycling** — spaced repetition across units

## Phrasal Verbs Bank

Organised by semantic field, covering high-frequency C1 phrasal verbs with formal alternatives.

| Phrasal Verb | Formal Equivalent | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *bring about* | cause, produce | The reforms brought about significant change. |
| *carry out* | conduct, execute | The study was carried out over two years. |
| *come up with* | devise, formulate | She came up with an innovative solution. |
| *draw on* | utilise, exploit | The author draws on personal experience. |
| *give rise to* | generate, produce | Inequality gives rise to social tension. |
| *look into* | investigate, examine | The committee will look into the allegations. |
| *put forward* | propose, suggest | She put forward a compelling argument. |
| *rule out* | exclude, eliminate | We cannot rule out human error. |
| *set out* | intend, aim | The report sets out three key recommendations. |
| *take into account* | consider, factor in | We must take economic factors into account. |
| *account for* | explain, constitute | This accounts for the sharp decline. |
| *build on* | expand, develop | We should build on previous research. |
| *stem from* | originate from, be caused by | The problem stems from poor regulation. |
| *stand for* | represent, signify | What does this acronym stand for? |
| *call for* | demand, require | The situation calls for immediate action. |
| *point to* | indicate, suggest | The data points to a systemic failure. |
| *live up to* | fulfil, meet | The product failed to live up to expectations. |
| *fall back on* | resort to, rely on | We can always fall back on the original plan. |
| *give way to* | be replaced by, yield to | Print has given way to digital media. |
| *bear out* | confirm, substantiate | The results bear out the initial hypothesis. |
