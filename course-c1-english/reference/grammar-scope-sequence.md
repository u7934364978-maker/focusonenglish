# Grammar Scope and Sequence — C1 English Course

## How to Read This Document

Each row maps a **unit** to its **primary grammar structure**, with:
- **Form** — the structural pattern
- **Key examples** — model sentences at C1 level
- **Progression rationale** — why this structure appears at this point in the course

## MODULE 1 — Identity and Society (Units 1–6)

*Phase: Activation — recalibrating B2 knowledge to C1 precision*

| Unit | Title | Grammar Structure | Form | Key Examples | Progression Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Personal Identity | Modal verbs — deduction & probability | *must/can't/could/might/should have + past participle* | *She must have known. He can't have been serious.* | Reactivates core modals; adds nuance of certainty degree |

## Grammar Progression Summary

### Phase 1 — Activation (Units 1–12, Modules 1–2)
Reactivates and refines B2 grammar, adding C1 precision:
- Modal verbs with nuanced probability and criticism
- Perfect aspect distinctions
- Reported speech with complex verb patterns
- Advanced passive and causative constructions
- Cleft sentences and inversion for emphasis
- Complex noun phrases and discourse markers

### Phase 2 — Consolidation (Units 13–30, Modules 3–5)
Introduces new C1 structures and strengthens formal register:
- Full conditional system (mixed and formal inversions)
- Advanced future forms and participle clauses
- Wish/regret structures
- Advanced relative clauses and absolute constructions
- Hedging and academic distancing
- Narrative tense precision and nominalisation

### Phase 3 — Refinement (Units 31–54, Modules 6–9)
Applies grammar to professional, academic and critical contexts:
- Professional conditionals and modals
- Nominal clauses and advanced reporting verbs
- Concession, contrast and cause/result text linkers
- Fronting, topicalisation and emphasis beyond inversions
- Speculation, distancing and impersonal constructions
- Mixed structure integration for stylistic sophistication

### Phase 4 — Synthesis (Units 55–60, Module 10)
Integrates all structures for certification and authentic production:
- Stylistic sentence variety
- Figurative language and register control
- Complex logical conjunctions
- Abstract nominalisation
- Full exam preparation and error awareness
