# Evaluation Criteria — C1 English Course

> **CEFR Level:** C1 Advanced  
> **Framework:** Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR)  
> **Exam alignment:** Cambridge C1 Advanced (CAE) · IELTS Academic 7.0+ · TOEFL iBT 100+

---

## Assessment Structure

| Component | Weight | Assessed in |
|---|---|---|
| **Reading** | 20% | Lesson L1 tasks + module reviews |
| **Listening** | 20% | Lesson L2 tasks + module reviews |
| **Writing** | 25% | Lesson L5 tasks + module reviews + end-of-module |
| **Speaking** | 20% | Lesson L6 tasks + module oral assessments |
| **Grammar & Vocabulary** | 15% | Lesson L3/L4 tasks + module consolidation tests |

## Reading Assessment

### CEFR C1 Reading Descriptors

> *Can understand long, complex factual and literary texts, appreciating distinctions of style. Can understand specialised articles and longer technical instructions. Can identify main conclusions in argumentative texts and recognise the line of argument. Can understand implicit attitudes, opinions and irony in complex texts.*

### Reading Rubric

| Band | Score | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **Excellent** | 5 | Fully understands main ideas, supporting details, implicit meaning, author attitude, and tone. Handles lexical and syntactic complexity without difficulty. Identifies irony, bias and inference precisely. |
| **Strong** | 4 | Understands most main ideas and details including implicit meaning. Minor difficulty with stylistic subtlety or highly specialised vocabulary. |
| **Satisfactory** | 3 | Understands main ideas and explicit information. Some difficulty with implicit meaning, tone, or complex argumentation. May misinterpret nuance. |
| **Developing** | 2 | Understands basic information and some detail. Frequent difficulty with inference, attitude, and complex text structure. |
| **Insufficient** | 1 | Limited comprehension. Struggles with most tasks beyond surface-level information. |

### Task Types and Objectives

| Task Type | C1 Skill Assessed |
|---|---|
| Multiple choice (4 options) | Global and detailed comprehension |
| True / False / Not Given | Implicit meaning and factual precision |
| Matching headings/information | Text organisation and skimming |
| Gap fill (lexical/grammatical) | Vocabulary in context |
| Short answer (in own words) | Detailed comprehension and paraphrase |
| Critical response (open-ended) | Inference, attitude, and analytical reading |
| Author viewpoint questions | Tone, irony, bias recognition |

## Listening Assessment

### CEFR C1 Listening Descriptors

> *Can understand extended speech even when it is not clearly structured and when relationships are only implied. Can understand television programmes and films with ease. Can follow extended speech and complex lines of argument provided the topic is reasonably familiar. Can understand a wide range of idiomatic expressions and colloquialisms, appreciating shifts in register.*

### Listening Rubric

| Band | Score | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **Excellent** | 5 | Fully understands extended, unscripted speech including implicit content, attitude, register shifts, and idiomatic expressions. Can extract and synthesise information from complex sources. |
| **Strong** | 4 | Understands most extended speech including implied content. Occasional difficulty with very fast or highly colloquial language. |
| **Satisfactory** | 3 | Understands main points and most details. Some difficulty with implicit meaning, shifts in speaker attitude, or rapidly delivered content. |
| **Developing** | 2 | Understands basic content and explicit information. Frequently misses implied content, attitude or register nuance. |
| **Insufficient** | 1 | Can only extract very limited information. Struggles with extended or unscripted speech. |

### Task Types and Objectives

| Task Type | C1 Skill Assessed |
|---|---|
| Multiple choice | Main point and detail comprehension |
| Note/sentence completion | Specific detail and paraphrase |
| Short answer | Detail comprehension |
| Speaker matching | Identification of attitude and opinion |
| Summary completion | Global understanding and synthesis |
| Inference questions | Implicit meaning, tone, attitude |
| Multiple speakers (debate/panel) | Tracking multiple perspectives |

## Writing Assessment

### CEFR C1 Writing Descriptors

> *Can write clear, well-structured expositions of complex subjects, underlining relevant salient issues. Can write different kinds of texts in a consistent, appropriate style with accurate grammar. Can write summaries of professional or literary texts, building on key points and arguments. Can produce a well-argued essay presenting a case, giving reasons in support of or against a particular point of view.*

### Writing Rubric — General (all task types)

| Criterion | Excellent (5) | Strong (4) | Satisfactory (3) | Developing (2) | Insufficient (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Task Achievement** | Fully addresses all requirements; content is relevant, developed and focused | Addresses requirements well; occasional underdevelopment | Addresses most requirements; some irrelevance or limited development | Partially addresses task; notable gaps or irrelevance | Fails to adequately address the task |
| **Coherence & Cohesion** | Logically organised with seamless use of cohesive devices; clear paragraph structure | Well-organised; generally effective use of cohesion with minor lapses | Adequate organisation; some incoherence or overuse/underuse of cohesive devices | Limited organisation; cohesive devices used inaccurately or mechanically | Poorly organised; ideas are unclear or disconnected |
| **Lexical Resource** | Wide range of sophisticated vocabulary used accurately; precise collocations; C1 AWL integration | Good range; some imprecision in choice; minor errors | Adequate range; errors in word choice/collocation; limited variety | Limited range; frequent errors; over-reliance on basic vocabulary | Very limited vocabulary; pervasive errors |
| **Grammatical Accuracy & Range** | Wide range of complex structures used with consistent accuracy; C1 grammar used naturally | Good range; occasional errors that do not impede communication | Some range; errors sometimes impede communication | Limited range; frequent errors | Very limited range; pervasive errors impede communication |
| **Register & Style** | Fully appropriate register for task type; consistent tone; stylistically accomplished | Generally appropriate register with occasional lapses | Attempts appropriate register; inconsistency in tone or style | Inconsistent register; mixed formal/informal inappropriately | Register not appropriate for task type |

## Speaking Assessment

### CEFR C1 Speaking Descriptors

> *Can express him/herself fluently and spontaneously, almost effortlessly. Can use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic and professional purposes. Can formulate ideas and opinions with precision and relate his/her contribution skillfully to those of other speakers. Can give clear, well-structured presentations on complex subjects, expanding and supporting points of view at some length with subsidiary points, reasons and relevant examples.*

### Speaking Rubric

| Criterion | Excellent (5) | Strong (4) | Satisfactory (3) | Developing (2) | Insufficient (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fluency & Spontaneity** | Speaks with natural rhythm and flow; minimal hesitation; repairs naturally | Fluent with occasional hesitation; generally smooth delivery | Generally communicates without major interruption; some notable pauses | Frequent hesitation and pausing; delivery often impeded | Very halting delivery; communication frequently breaks down |
| **Lexical Range & Precision** | Wide range of precise vocabulary; effective use of idiom, collocation and AWL | Good range; occasionally imprecise or repetitive | Adequate range; some imprecision; limited variety | Limited range; frequent repetition or imprecision | Very limited vocabulary; seriously impedes communication |
| **Grammatical Range & Accuracy** | Wide range of complex structures; errors rare and self-corrected | Good range; occasional errors that do not impede | Some complex structures; errors sometimes impede | Limited to simple structures; frequent errors | Very limited range; pervasive errors |
| **Pronunciation & Delivery** | Clear, natural pronunciation; effective use of stress, rhythm and intonation | Generally clear; occasional pronunciation issues that rarely impede | Mostly intelligible; some sound/stress errors that occasionally impede | Pronunciation errors regularly impede communication | Very difficult to understand |
| **Interactive Competence** | Initiates, maintains and closes interaction skillfully; responds precisely to other speakers | Good interaction; generally responsive; occasional missed nuance | Participates adequately; sometimes fails to build on partner's contributions | Limited interaction; mostly listens or gives minimal responses | Unable to sustain interaction effectively |
| **Task Achievement** | Fully develops ideas with examples and reasons; addresses all aspects of the task | Addresses most aspects; some underdevelopment | Addresses main aspects; some gaps or lack of development | Partially addresses task; significant gaps | Fails to adequately address the task |

### Speaking Task Types

| Task Type | Duration | Module |
|---|---|---|
| Structured debate (with assigned position) | 5–7 min (pair) | M1, M3, M8 |
| Individual presentation (with Q&A) | 3–4 min monologue + 2 min Q&A | M2, M5, M9 |
| Collaborative task (problem-solving) | 4–5 min (pair) | M4, M6 |
| Role-play (professional scenario) | 4–5 min (pair) | M3, M6 |
| Extended monologue (spontaneous) | 2–3 min (individual) | M7, M10 |
| Oral module assessment | 8–10 min | Every module review unit |
| Mock CAE Speaking test | 15 min (pair) | Unit 60 |

## Grammar & Vocabulary Assessment

### CEFR C1 Grammar & Vocabulary Descriptors

> *Maintains consistent grammatical control of complex language, even while attention is otherwise engaged. Uses language with flexibility and precision. Controls a large repertoire of complex grammatical structures with infrequent slips or non-systematic errors.*

### Grammar Rubric

| Band | Score | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **Excellent** | 5 | Consistent control of a wide range of complex structures. Errors are rare, non-systematic, and self-corrected. Uses C1 grammar naturally (inversion, cleft, mixed conditionals, participle clauses, etc.). |
| **Strong** | 4 | Good control of complex structures with occasional errors. Range is C1 but slightly inconsistent under pressure. |
| **Satisfactory** | 3 | Adequate control of moderately complex structures. Some systematic errors in C1 structures. B2 structures generally accurate. |
| **Developing** | 2 | Limited to a narrower range; frequent errors in complex structures; may rely on B2 forms. |
| **Insufficient** | 1 | Very limited range; pervasive errors; communication impeded. |

### Grammar Assessment Task Types

| Task Type | Skill Assessed |
|---|---|
| Error correction (identify and correct) | Accuracy awareness |
| Sentence transformation (CAE-style) | Structural flexibility and range |
| Gap fill (grammatical forms) | Form control in context |
| Open gap fill (own words) | Grammatical and lexical production |
| Register transfer (informal → formal) | Register control and grammatical range |
| Directed writing (using given structures) | Controlled production |
| Self-assessment grammar checklist | Metacognitive awareness |

### Module Consolidation Tests

Each module review unit (Units 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36, 42, 48, 54, 60) includes a 25-item grammar consolidation test covering all structures from that module.

| Item Type | Number per test |
|---|---|
| Multiple choice (grammar) | 8 items |
| Sentence transformation | 8 items |
| Error identification | 5 items |
| Open gap fill | 4 items |
| **Total** | **25 items** |

**Scoring:**
- 23–25: Excellent — C1 control confirmed
- 19–22: Strong — C1 emerging
- 15–18: Satisfactory — B2+ consolidated
- 11–14: Developing — requires review
- 0–10: Insufficient — restudy required before progressing

## Can-Do Checklist — End of Course

By the end of Unit 60, students should be able to confirm **all of the following**:

### Reading
- [ ] I can understand long, complex texts including literary and academic texts
- [ ] I can identify implicit meaning, attitude, tone and irony in complex texts
- [ ] I can follow the line of argument in discursive and persuasive texts
- [ ] I can understand specialised vocabulary in context

### Listening
- [ ] I can understand extended, unscripted speech including when structure is unclear
- [ ] I can follow complex lines of argument in lectures, debates and discussions
- [ ] I can understand a wide range of idioms and register shifts
- [ ] I can identify speaker attitude, opinion and implied meaning

### Writing
- [ ] I can produce well-structured, well-argued essays on complex topics
- [ ] I can write in different genres (essay, report, article, proposal, letter) in an appropriate style
- [ ] I can use a wide range of vocabulary and grammar structures accurately
- [ ] I can write under timed conditions (e.g. 45–60 minutes per task)

### Speaking
- [ ] I can speak fluently and spontaneously with minimal hesitation
- [ ] I can give structured presentations and monologues on complex topics
- [ ] I can participate effectively in debates, discussions and role-plays
- [ ] I can interact naturally, responding precisely to what other speakers say

### Grammar & Vocabulary
- [ ] I can use a wide range of complex grammatical structures with consistent accuracy
- [ ] I can use sophisticated vocabulary, collocations and idioms naturally
- [ ] I can vary my style and register appropriately for the context
- [ ] I can identify and self-correct my own errors
