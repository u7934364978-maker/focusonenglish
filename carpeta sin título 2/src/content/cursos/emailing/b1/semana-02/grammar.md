# Grammar (B1) · Semana 02

> Objetivo: hacer follow-ups neutrales y pedir updates sin sonar insistente, usando preguntas educadas + “next steps”.

## 1) Follow-up neutro (sin acusar)

| Intención | Patrón (EN) | Ejemplo (EN) |
|---|---|---|
| Follow-up suave | Just checking if + clause | Just checking if you had a chance to review this. |
| Follow-up formal | I wanted to follow up on + noun | I wanted to follow up on my last email. |
| Update neutro | Any updates on + noun? | Any updates on the timeline? |
| Pregunta educada | Could you let me know + clause? | Could you let me know when you expect to decide? |

> Regla rápida: “Just checking…” = tono neutro (no culpa). Evita “Why haven’t you replied?”

**✅ Correcto**
- Just checking if you had a chance to review this.
- I wanted to follow up on my previous message.

**❌ Incorrecto**
- Why didn’t you reply?
- You must answer ASAP.

---

## 2) Pedir un update + proponer una fecha (sin imponer)

| Uso | Patrón (EN) | Ejemplo (EN) |
|---|---|---|
| Pedir update | Do you have any updates on…? | Do you have any updates on the draft approval? |
| Proponer fecha | Would it be possible to + VERB + by + day/date? | Would it be possible to confirm by Thursday? |
| Opción “suave” | If you need more time, that’s fine. | If you need more time, that’s fine. |

> “Would it be possible…?” es B1 y suena profesional.

---

## 3) Next steps (cerrar con acción clara)

- The next step would be to approve the final version.
- Once confirmed, I’ll share the final file.
- If that works for you, I’ll proceed.

> Tip: “Once + past participle” (Once confirmed / Once approved) = muy útil para emails.
