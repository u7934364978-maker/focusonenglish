# Grammar (B1) · Semana 03

> Objetivo: proponer horarios, pedir disponibilidad y reprogramar sin sonar agresivo.

## 1) Proponer horarios (opciones claras)

| Intención | Patrón (EN) | Ejemplo (EN) |
|---|---|---|
| Proponer 2–3 opciones | Would [day/time] work for you? | Would Tuesday at 11:00 work for you? |
| Ofrecer alternativas | I’m available [A], [B], or [C]. | I’m available Tue 11:00, Wed 15:00, or Thu 10:30. |
| Preguntar disponibilidad | When are you available? | When are you available this week? |

> Tip: 2–3 opciones = menos idas y vueltas que “When are you free?”

---

## 2) Reprogramar con tono neutro

| Intención | Patrón (EN) | Ejemplo (EN) |
|---|---|---|
| Reprogramar | Would it be possible to reschedule? | Would it be possible to reschedule our call? |
| Disculpa breve | Sorry for the change. | Sorry for the change—something came up. |
| Proponer nueva hora | Could we move it to…? | Could we move it to Thursday at 10:00? |

**✅ Correcto**
- Would it be possible to reschedule our call?
- Sorry for the change. Could we move it to Thursday at 10:00?

**❌ Incorrecto**
- We must reschedule. (suena duro)
- Change it now. (imperativo)

---

## 3) Confirmar + siguiente paso

- Great — I’ll send a calendar invite.
- Once confirmed, I’ll share the meeting link.
- Please let me know which option works best.

> Regla rápida: termina con una acción clara (invite/link) para cerrar el hilo.
