Cuando alguien lleva años escribiendo emails en inglés, lo que diferencia sus correos no es un vocabulario excepcional: es el dominio de un repertorio de frases que usa de forma natural según la situación. Este es ese repertorio, organizado por funciones, para que lo tengas siempre a mano.

## Aperturas: cómo empezar el correo

**Para contactos nuevos o contextos muy formales:**
- *I hope this email finds you well.*
- *My name is [nombre] and I am [cargo] at [empresa].*
- *I am writing to you on behalf of [empresa/departamento].*
- *I was given your contact details by [nombre].*

**Para responder a un correo:**
- *Thank you for your email of [fecha].*
- *Thank you for getting back to me so quickly.*
- *Many thanks for your prompt reply.*
- *Further to our conversation earlier today...*

**Para reanudar el contacto:**
- *I hope you have been well since we last spoke.*
- *I am following up on my previous email dated [fecha].*
- *I wanted to get back to you regarding [tema].*

**Para referirse a una reunión o evento anterior:**
- *It was a pleasure meeting you at [evento].*
- *Following our meeting on [fecha], I wanted to...*
- *As we discussed during our call on Thursday...*

El hub [Emails Profesionales en Inglés](/hubs/emails-profesionales-ingles) tiene guías específicas para cada tipo de correo con estructuras completas.

## Expresar el propósito: ir al grano con elegancia

- *I am writing to enquire about / inform you of / request...*
- *The purpose of this email is to...*
- *I would like to bring to your attention...*
- *I am contacting you regarding [tema].*
- *I am writing in connection with [proyecto/oferta].*
- *This email is to confirm / update you on / let you know that...*

## Hacer peticiones y solicitudes

**Peticiones directas pero educadas:**
- *Could you please [acción] by [fecha]?*
- *Would you be able to [acción]?*
- *I would appreciate it if you could [acción].*
- *Please could you [acción] at your earliest convenience?*
- *If possible, could you [acción] before [fecha]?*

**Para pedir información o documentos:**
- *Could you send me [documento/información]?*
- *Would it be possible to forward me the [informe]?*
- *I would be grateful if you could provide me with [datos].*
- *Please find attached [documento]. I would welcome your feedback.*

**Para solicitar una reunión:**
- *Would you be available for a [30-minute] call next week?*
- *I was wondering if we could arrange a meeting to discuss [tema].*
- *Are you free on [día] at [hora]?*
- *Please let me know what time would suit you.*

## Dar información y actualizar

- *I am pleased to inform you that...*
- *I am writing to let you know that...*
- *Please be advised that...*
- *I would like to update you on [proyecto].*
- *As previously mentioned, [información].*
- *For your information / FYI, [detalle].*
- *Please note that [cambio/condición].*

## Adjuntar documentos

- *Please find attached [nombre del documento].*
- *I have attached [documento] for your reference.*
- *Enclosed please find [documento].* (más formal, para cartas)
- *Please see the attached [documento].*
- *Let me know if you have any trouble opening the file.*

## Disculparse y gestionar problemas

- *I apologize for the inconvenience / the delay.*
- *I am sorry for any confusion this may have caused.*
- *Please accept my sincere apologies for [error].*
- *I regret to inform you that [problema].*
- *I take full responsibility for [error].*
- *We are looking into this matter and will get back to you shortly.*

## Dar malas noticias con diplomacia

- *Unfortunately, I am unable to [acción] at this time.*
- *I regret to inform you that [mala noticia].*
- *I am afraid we cannot [acción] due to [razón].*
- *After careful consideration, we have decided not to [acción].*
- *While I appreciate your interest, I am afraid we cannot...*

## Confirmar, aceptar y agradecer

- *I can confirm that [detalle].*
- *I am happy to confirm our meeting on [fecha] at [hora].*
- *Thank you for the opportunity to [presentar/colaborar].*
- *I am delighted to accept your offer.*
- *That works perfectly for me.*
- *I will proceed as discussed.*

## Cierres: terminar con intención

- *I look forward to hearing from you.*
- *I look forward to meeting you on [fecha].*
- *Please do not hesitate to contact me if you have any questions.*
- *I await your response.*
- *I welcome any feedback you might have.*
- *Please let me know if there is anything else I can help with.*
- *I will be in touch shortly.*

## Despedidas (sign-offs)

| Formal | Profesional estándar | Más cercano |
|---|---|---|
| *Yours sincerely,* | *Best regards,* | *Best,* |
| *Yours faithfully,* | *Kind regards,* | *Thanks,* |
| *Respectfully,* | *Regards,* | *Cheers,* (UK informal) |

## Frases que conviene evitar

Algunas expresiones suenan pasivo-agresivas o anticuadas en inglés profesional actual:

- ❌ *As per my last email* → suena a reproche
- ❌ *Please revert* → anglicismo indio, no es estándar en inglés occidental
- ❌ *Kindly do the needful* → igualmente, regionalismo que puede sonar raro
- ❌ *I send you a greeting* → traducción literal incorrecta
- ❌ *Sorry for bother you* → forma incorrecta ("for bothering you")
- ❌ *Hoping to hear from you* → incompleto, mejor *I look forward to hearing from you*

Con este repertorio cubierto, lo que necesitas es practicar usándolo en situaciones reales. Guarda este artículo como referencia y consúltalo antes de redactar correos importantes hasta que las frases fluyan de forma automática.
