La negociación por email en inglés requiere un equilibrio preciso: ser claro sin ser agresivo, firme sin ser rígido, y cortés sin parecer débil. La buena noticia es que hay estructuras y frases que funcionan casi siempre.

## Antes de escribir: lo que debes tener claro

Antes de empezar a teclear, necesitas tener definidos tres elementos:

1. **Tu objetivo ideal:** lo que quieres conseguir
2. **Tu zona de aceptación:** hasta dónde puedes ceder
3. **Tu alternativa si no hay acuerdo:** qué pasa si negocias sin éxito

Eso determina tu tono. Si tienes alternativas, puedes ser más firme. Si necesitas este acuerdo, tendrás que ser más flexible y estratégico.

## Hacer una propuesta inicial

El primer email de una negociación debe incluir:
- Contexto claro (quién eres, qué propones)
- La propuesta concreta (precio, plazo, condiciones)
- Una razón por la que es una buena oferta para ambos
- Una llamada a la acción clara

**Frases para abrir:**
- *I would like to propose the following terms for our collaboration.*
- *Following our conversation, I am pleased to put forward a formal proposal.*
- *We are interested in moving forward and would like to suggest the following arrangement.*

**Para presentar términos:**
- *We are proposing a fee of [cantidad] for [servicio/producto].*
- *The initial contract term would be [duración], renewable upon mutual agreement.*
- *We would require a [porcentaje]% deposit, with the remainder due upon completion.*

## Responder a una propuesta

Cuando recibes una propuesta y quieres negociar, no respondas con un "no" directo. Usa el método de reconocer y redirigir:

- *Thank you for your proposal. We are very interested in working with you.*
- *I have reviewed your offer carefully and would like to discuss a few points.*
- *The proposal is largely in line with what we had in mind, though there are a couple of areas I'd like to explore further.*

## Pedir concesiones o un descuento

Esta es la parte que más incomoda a los hispanohablantes, pero es completamente normal en inglés profesional:

- *Would there be any flexibility on the pricing?*
- *Given the volume we are committing to, would you be able to offer a preferential rate?*
- *We have a slightly tighter budget than [cifra]. Is there a way to adjust the package to fit within that range?*
- *If we were to extend the contract to [período], could you offer a better rate?*
- *We're comparing several suppliers at the moment. Your proposal is very competitive, but there's a gap of approximately [cantidad]. Is there room to close that?*

## Hacer una contraoferta

Una contraoferta bien estructurada acepta parte de la propuesta original y modifica lo que no te conviene:

- *We accept the proposed timeline and are happy to proceed on those terms. However, with regard to the pricing, we would be more comfortable with [tu cifra].*
- *Your terms around delivery work well for us. We would like to propose a payment schedule of [condiciones] instead of the upfront payment.*
- *We are aligned on [punto acordado]. On the question of [punto en disputa], our position is [tu propuesta].*

## Gestionar la presión y los plazos

A veces el otro lado pone presión artificial con plazos o ultimátums. Puedes responder sin perder la calma:

- *I understand there's some urgency on your side. Let me get back to you by [fecha] with our final position.*
- *We are close to a decision and expect to be able to confirm by [fecha].*
- *We appreciate the offer, but we are not in a position to commit before [fecha], as we need to complete our internal review.*

## Llegar a un acuerdo: frases de cierre

Cuando ambas partes están de acuerdo, es importante confirmarlo por escrito de forma clara:

- *I am pleased to confirm that we have reached an agreement on the following terms:*
- *This email confirms our mutual agreement on [resumen de términos].*
- *We are happy to proceed on those terms. Please send across the contract when ready.*
- *I look forward to a successful collaboration.*

## Ejemplo: contraoferta de precio

> **Subject:** Re: Proposal for Content Translation Services — Revised Terms
>
> Dear Ms. Okafor,
>
> Thank you for sending through your proposal. We were impressed with your portfolio and are genuinely interested in working with your team.
>
> Having reviewed the terms, we are fully aligned on the timeline and the revision process you outlined. Regarding the pricing, however, our budget for this project is capped at €4,500, which is somewhat below your proposed fee of €5,800.
>
> We understand this represents a reduction, and we want to be transparent about our constraints. To make the project viable on both sides, we would be open to slightly reducing the word count for the initial phase, focusing on the highest-priority materials.
>
> Would you be willing to explore this adjustment? We'd welcome a brief call this week if that would help us find common ground.
>
> Kind regards,
> Marco Esteve
> Content Manager, Nordik Digital

Este email reconoce el valor de la propuesta, explica la restricción con honestidad, ofrece una solución alternativa y abre la puerta al diálogo sin presionar. Es el equilibrio correcto.

La negociación por email en inglés se aprende con práctica, pero una vez que interiorizas estas estructuras, descubrirás que el inglés profesional tiene convenciones muy claras que hacen que el proceso sea más predecible de lo que parece.
