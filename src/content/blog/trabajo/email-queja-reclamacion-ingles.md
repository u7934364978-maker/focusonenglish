Poner una queja en inglés tiene su arte. Si el tono es demasiado suave, el correo se archiva y no pasa nada. Si es demasiado agresivo, la otra parte se pone a la defensiva y la solución tarda más. El punto es encontrar el registro firme y profesional que lleve al destinatario a actuar.

## La lógica de un buen complaint email

Un email de queja efectivo tiene cuatro elementos:

1. **Explicar claramente qué ocurrió** (con datos, fechas y referencias)
2. **Describir el impacto** (por qué es un problema real)
3. **Pedir una solución concreta** (no solo quejarse)
4. **Dar un plazo razonable**

Sin estos cuatro elementos, el correo pierde efectividad.

## El asunto (subject line)

El asunto debe identificar claramente que es una queja y sobre qué, sin ser amenazante:

- *Complaint: Damaged Goods — Order #48291*
- *Formal Complaint: Service Failure on 5 March*
- *Unsatisfactory Response — Invoice #2034*
- *Issue with [Producto/Servicio] — Request for Resolution*

Evita asuntos vagos como *Problem* o *Important* y evita también los que suenan agresivos desde el asunto: no necesitas ir a la guerra antes de que te lean.

## El saludo y la apertura

En un email de queja, la apertura contextualiza y establece el propósito:

- *I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with [producto/servicio].*
- *I am writing to formally complain about [problema].*
- *I am contacting you regarding an issue with [detalle] that has not been resolved.*
- *Following my purchase of [producto] on [fecha], I have encountered a serious problem.*

Si ya hay una referencia previa (número de pedido, ticket de soporte), menciónala en la apertura.

## Describir el problema: hechos, no emociones

Esta es la parte más importante. Describe lo que ocurrió con precisión:

- *On [fecha], I ordered [producto] via your website (Order #XXXX).*
- *Upon delivery, I discovered that [descripción del defecto/problema].*
- *Despite contacting your customer service team on [fecha], the issue remains unresolved.*
- *The [producto/servicio] does not meet the specifications outlined in your description.*
- *This is the third time this issue has occurred, despite assurances from your team.*

Usa fechas específicas, números de referencia y datos concretos. Eso da peso a la queja y dificulta que te la rechacen por falta de información.

En el hub [Emails Profesionales en Inglés](/hubs/emails-profesionales-ingles) encontrarás plantillas completas para este y otros tipos de correo.

## Describir el impacto

Explica brevemente por qué el problema te afecta. Esto no es para dramatizar; es para que la otra parte entienda la urgencia:

- *As a result, I have been unable to [consecuencia].*
- *This has caused significant inconvenience / financial loss / delays to our project.*
- *Due to this issue, we have missed the agreed deadline.*
- *This situation is affecting our ability to serve our own clients.*

## Pedir una solución concreta

No termines con un vago "espero que lo solucionen." Di exactamente qué quieres:

- *I would like to request a full refund of [cantidad].*
- *I am requesting that the item be replaced at no additional cost.*
- *I expect this issue to be resolved by [fecha].*
- *I would appreciate compensation for the inconvenience caused.*
- *I require written confirmation that this will not occur again.*
- *I am asking for a reduction in the invoice amount given the circumstances.*

## El tono: firme sin ser agresivo

El inglés profesional tiene recursos muy específicos para ser firme sin sonar hostil:

**Neutral:**
- *I am disappointed with the service I have received.*
- *This situation is unacceptable.*
- *I expected a higher standard of service.*

**Firme con advertencia:**
- *If this matter is not resolved by [fecha], I will have no choice but to escalate the complaint.*
- *Should I not receive a satisfactory response within [X] days, I will contact [autoridad/organismo regulador].*
- *I trust this matter will receive your urgent attention.*

Evita estas frases que restan profesionalidad:
- ❌ *This is outrageous!*
- ❌ *I demand you fix this immediately.*
- ❌ *You people are completely incompetent.*

## El cierre

- *I look forward to your prompt response.*
- *I await your response and trust that this matter will be resolved quickly.*
- *Please respond by [fecha] with a proposed solution.*

## Ejemplo completo

> **Subject:** Complaint: Damaged Item — Order #48291
>
> Dear Ms. Wilson,
>
> I am writing to formally complain about a recent purchase made through your online store.
>
> On 1 March 2026, I ordered a [Nombre del producto] (Order #48291). When the item arrived on 5 March, I found that the packaging was severely damaged and the product itself had a visible crack along the base, making it unusable.
>
> I contacted your customer service team by phone on 6 March and was told that a replacement would be sent within 5 working days. It is now 14 March and I have received no replacement or further communication.
>
> This is unacceptable, particularly as the product was intended as a gift for an event taking place on 20 March.
>
> I am requesting that a replacement be dispatched urgently or, alternatively, that I receive a full refund of €89.90. I would appreciate a response by 16 March confirming which course of action you intend to take.
>
> I look forward to hearing from you.
>
> Sincerely,
> Carmen Rodríguez

Este ejemplo tiene todos los elementos: contexto claro, datos concretos, impacto explicado, solución pedida y plazo. Ese es el estándar al que hay que aspirar.
