Si has intentado leer un artículo científico en inglés o escribir un ensayo académico, habrás notado algo: la voz pasiva está en todas partes. No es casualidad ni es un capricho estilístico. En el inglés académico, la pasiva cumple una función precisa: dar objetividad, centrar el foco en los procesos y resultados, y mantener el tono impersonal que se espera en la escritura científica y formal.

## Por qué la pasiva domina el inglés académico

En una conversación, cuando dices *"I found that..."* suena natural. Pero en un artículo de investigación, ese *"I"* repetido crea la sensación de que el trabajo es subjetivo y personal. La ciencia aspira a la objetividad, y la pasiva es el vehículo gramatical de esa objetividad:

- *It was found that stress levels increased significantly.*
- *A correlation was observed between sleep quality and academic performance.*
- *The samples were collected from three different regions.*

Nadie dice quién encontró, observó o recogió. El foco está en el hallazgo, no en el investigador.

## Estructuras clave en la metodología

La sección de metodología (*Methods* o *Methodology*) es donde la pasiva tiene más presencia. Describe lo que se hizo de forma sistemática y reproducible:

**Selección de participantes:**
- *Participants were selected using a stratified random sampling method.*
- *Sixty adults were recruited from three different universities.*
- *Volunteers were excluded if they had been diagnosed with anxiety disorders.*

**Diseño del estudio:**
- *A mixed-methods approach was adopted for this research.*
- *The study was designed to investigate the relationship between X and Y.*
- *Two groups were formed: a control group and an experimental group.*

**Recogida de datos:**
- *Data was collected over a period of twelve months.*
- *Questionnaires were distributed to all participants at the beginning and end of the study.*
- *Interviews were conducted in the participants' native language.*

**Análisis:**
- *The data was analyzed using SPSS version 26.*
- *Statistical significance was determined at p < 0.05.*
- *Thematic analysis was applied to the qualitative data.*

## Estructuras pasivas para resultados y conclusiones

En la sección de resultados (*Results*) y discusión (*Discussion*), la pasiva también es dominante:

**Para reportar hallazgos:**
- *A significant difference was found between the two groups.*
- *No correlation was observed between age and performance.*
- *Higher levels of engagement were reported among younger participants.*

**Para interpretar o comparar:**
- *These findings can be interpreted as evidence of...*
- *The results are consistent with those reported by Smith et al. (2020).*
- *Several limitations should be acknowledged.*

**Para concluir:**
- *It can be concluded that...*
- *Further research is needed to...*
- *These results suggest that more attention should be paid to...*

## La pasiva impersonal: el lenguaje de la cautela científica

Los académicos raramente afirman nada con total certeza. La pasiva impersonal (*It is/was + verbo de reporte*) es perfecta para esto:

- *It has been argued that...* (Se ha argumentado que...)
- *It is widely accepted that...* (Se acepta ampliamente que...)
- *It has been suggested that...* (Se ha sugerido que...)
- *It remains unclear whether...* (No está claro todavía si...)
- *It should be noted that...* (Cabe señalar que...)

Esta forma de escribir no es cobardía intelectual; es precisión. El científico distingue entre lo que sabe con certeza y lo que infiere o sugiere.

## Verbos más usados en pasiva académica

Algunos verbos aparecen con una frecuencia especialmente alta en textos académicos en pasiva:

| Verbo | Uso típico |
|---|---|
| find | *It was found that...* |
| observe | *X was observed in...* |
| suggest | *These results suggest...* |
| analyze | *The data was analyzed...* |
| collect | *Samples were collected...* |
| conduct | *The study was conducted...* |
| examine | *The issue was examined...* |
| determine | *The threshold was determined...* |
| calculate | *Values were calculated using...* |
| measure | *Variables were measured at...* |
| compare | *Groups were compared using...* |
| identify | *Patterns were identified in...* |

## Errores frecuentes que hay que evitar

**1. Mezclar personas sin criterio**

Pasar de pasiva a *"we did"* dentro del mismo párrafo crea inconsistencia:
- ❌ *The samples were analyzed. We then compared the results.*
- ✅ *The samples were analyzed. The results were then compared.*

**2. Pasiva donde no tiene sentido**

No toda oración necesita ser pasiva. Si el agente es importante y relevante, usa activa:
- ❌ *Einstein's theory of relativity was developed by Einstein in 1905.* (Redundante)
- ✅ *Einstein developed the theory of relativity in 1905.*

**3. Omitir el agente cuando es necesario**

A veces sí es importante especificar quién hizo algo:
- ✅ *The analysis was conducted by an independent research team.* (Añade credibilidad.)

**4. Participios irregulares incorrectos**

*"The data was showed"* → *"The data was shown"*. Los participios irregulares son un escollo frecuente.

## Un párrafo de metodología de ejemplo

> *The study was conducted between January and June 2024. Participants were recruited through online advertisements and were divided into two groups of thirty. Each participant was asked to complete a standardized anxiety questionnaire before and after the intervention. The results were recorded anonymously and were stored in a password-protected database. Statistical analysis was performed using R software (version 4.2.1). Significance was set at p < 0.05.*

Todo en pasiva. Todo objetivo. Todo en el tono que se espera de un texto científico en inglés.

Si estás escribiendo un TFG, un TFM o cualquier tipo de trabajo académico en inglés, interiorizar estas estructuras te dará una ventaja enorme. No es solo gramática: es el lenguaje que los evaluadores esperan ver.
