Si ya dominas el **Zero, First, Second y Third Conditional**, el siguiente paso es entender los **Mixed Conditionals** — estructuras que mezclan tiempos de diferentes condicionales para expresar relaciones más complejas entre el pasado y el presente. Son indispensables en el examen **Cambridge C1 Advanced** y en cualquier comunicación escrita sofisticada.

---

## ¿Qué son los Mixed Conditionals?

Los **Mixed Conditionals** combinan elementos de dos tipos de condicionales para hablar de:

1. **Un pasado irreal que tendría consecuencias en el presente** (Mixed Type A)
2. **Una condición presente irreal que habría tenido consecuencias en el pasado** (Mixed Type B)

---

## Mixed Type A: Pasado Irreal → Consecuencia Presente

**Estructura**: *If + past perfect, would + infinitivo (sin to)*

Usamos este tipo cuando queremos decir: *"Si algo hubiera pasado en el pasado (pero no pasó), las cosas serían diferentes ahora."*

### Ejemplos con análisis

| Frase | Análisis |
|-------|----------|
| *If I had studied medicine, I would be a doctor now.* | Pasado: no estudié medicina. Presente: no soy médico. |
| *If she hadn't moved abroad, she would still be living here.* | Pasado: se marchó al extranjero. Presente: ya no vive aquí. |
| *If they had invested in that startup, they would be millionaires today.* | Pasado: no invirtieron. Presente: no son millonarios. |
| *If he had taken better care of his health, he wouldn't feel so tired all the time.* | Pasado: descuidó su salud. Presente: se siente agotado. |

**Pista clave**: La cláusula principal tiene *would + infinitivo* (no *would have + participio*), porque el resultado se sitúa en el **presente**.

---

## Mixed Type B: Condición Presente Irreal → Consecuencia Pasada

**Estructura**: *If + past simple, would have + participio pasado*

Usamos este tipo cuando decimos: *"Si (ahora mismo) yo fuera diferente, entonces (en el pasado) las cosas habrían sido distintas."*

### Ejemplos con análisis

| Frase | Análisis |
|-------|----------|
| *If she were more ambitious, she would have applied for the director's position.* | Ahora: no es ambiciosa. Pasado: no solicitó el puesto. |
| *If he weren't so shy, he would have spoken to her at the party.* | Ahora: es tímido. Pasado: no habló con ella. |
| *If I were a morning person, I wouldn't have missed that early meeting.* | Ahora: no soy madrugador. Pasado: perdí la reunión. |
| *If they were more organized, they wouldn't have lost those files.* | Ahora: son desorganizados. Pasado: perdieron los archivos. |

**Pista clave**: La cláusula principal tiene *would have + participio* porque el resultado está en el **pasado**.

---

## Cómo Distinguirlos: El Truco del "Marcador Temporal"

Busca marcadores temporales para identificar el tipo:

- **Mixed Type A**: suele aparecer **now**, **today**, **still**, **at this moment** en la cláusula resultado.
  - *...she would be working here **now**.*
  
- **Mixed Type B**: suele haber referencia a un **momento pasado específico** en la cláusula resultado.
  - *...she wouldn't have said that **at the interview**.*

## Variaciones y Matices Avanzados

### Con otros modales (could, might)

Los Mixed Conditionals también funcionan con **could** y **might** para expresar posibilidad o capacidad:

- *If she had taken that course, she could be applying for senior positions now.*
- *If he hadn't been so reckless, he might still have his driving licence.*

### Inversión formal (sin "if")

En inglés formal y escrito, podemos invertir el sujeto y el auxiliar eliminando *if*:

- *Had I studied harder, I would be in a better position today.* (= If I had studied harder...)
- *Were she more experienced, she wouldn't have made those errors.* (= If she were more experienced...)

Esta inversión es muy común en textos académicos, legales y literarios.

## Ejercicios: Completa los Mixed Conditionals

### Ejercicio 1: Mixed Type A (pasado → presente)

1. If he _______ (choose) a different career, he _______ (be) happier now.
2. If they _______ (not move) to London, they _______ (still live) in Barcelona.
3. If I _______ (learn) Chinese earlier, I _______ (have) more job opportunities today.

**Soluciones:**
1. had chosen / would be
2. hadn't moved / would still be living
3. had learned / would have

### Ejercicio 2: Mixed Type B (presente → pasado)

1. If she _______ (be) more patient, she _______ (not quit) her job last month.
2. If he _______ (not be) so proud, he _______ (ask) for help earlier.
3. If I _______ (be) better at managing stress, I _______ (not make) those mistakes.

**Soluciones:**
1. were / wouldn't have quit
2. weren't / would have asked
3. were / wouldn't have made

### Ejercicio 3: Identifica el tipo correcto

Indica si cada frase es **Mixed Type A** o **Mixed Type B**:

1. *If you had listened to me, you wouldn't be in this mess now.*
2. *If he weren't so arrogant, he would have apologized.*
3. *If she had taken the scholarship, she might be studying at Oxford today.*

**Respuestas:**
1. Mixed Type A
2. Mixed Type B
3. Mixed Type A

---

## Errores Comunes a Evitar

| Error | Corrección |
|-------|------------|
| *If I had studied, I would have been a doctor now.* | *If I had studied, I would **be** a doctor now.* |
| *If she was more confident, she would have succeeded.* | *If she **were** more confident, she would have succeeded.* |
| *Had I known, I would have acted different.* | *Had I known, I would have acted **differently**.* |

## Aplicación en Cambridge C1/C2

En el **Part 4 de Use of English** de Cambridge C1 Advanced, los Mixed Conditionals aparecen con frecuencia en Key Word Transformation. Practica transformaciones como:

- *She didn't prepare adequately, so she can't do the job well now.* (HAD)
  → *If she **had** prepared adequately, she would be able to do the job well now.*

- *He is lazy, and that's why he failed the exam.* (WERE)
  → *If he **were** less lazy, he wouldn't have failed the exam.*

Los **Mixed Conditionals** son una de las estructuras que más distinguen a un hablante avanzado de uno intermedio. Con práctica sistemática, estos patrones se vuelven automáticos y enriquecen enormemente tu inglés escrito y oral.
