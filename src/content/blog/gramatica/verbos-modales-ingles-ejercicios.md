Dominar los **verbos modales en inglés** requiere práctica sistemática. En esta guía de ejercicios, trabajaremos desde los usos más básicos hasta las construcciones avanzadas con Modales Perfectos.

Si primero quieres repasar los conceptos teóricos, la guía [Verbos Modales en Inglés](/hubs/verbos-modales-ingles) tiene todos los usos explicados con ejemplos.

---

## Bloque 1 — Ejercicios de Nivel A2/B1: Habilidad y Permiso

Los modales **can**, **could** y **may** expresan habilidad, posibilidad y permiso.

### Ejercicio 1: Completa con can, can't, could o couldn't

1. When I was a child, I _______ swim, but now I _______ swim very well.
2. _______ you help me with this report, please?
3. She _______ come to the meeting yesterday; she was ill.
4. _______ I use your pen for a moment?
5. He _______ speak three languages by the time he was ten.

**Soluciones:**
1. couldn't / can
2. Could
3. couldn't
4. Can / May
5. could

---

## Bloque 2 — Ejercicios de Nivel B1/B2: Obligación y Consejo

### Ejercicio 2: Must, Have to, Should, Ought to

Elige el modal más adecuado según el contexto:

1. You _______ wear a seatbelt in a car. (obligación legal)
2. You _______ see a doctor. You don't look well. (consejo)
3. I _______ finish this project by Monday. (obligación personal)
4. He _______ be more careful with his emails. (consejo formal)
5. We _______ not eat in the laboratory. (prohibición)

**Soluciones:**
1. must / have to
2. should / ought to
3. must / have to
4. should / ought to
5. must

---

## Bloque 3 — Ejercicios de Nivel B2: Posibilidad y Deducción

Los modales **might**, **may**, **must**, **can't** expresan diferentes grados de certeza.

### Ejercicio 3: Deducciones en presente

Observa la situación y forma una deducción lógica:

1. The lights are off and there's no car in the drive. → They _______ be at home. (imposible)
2. She's been working for 12 hours straight. → She _______ be exhausted. (casi seguro)
3. I'm not sure, but he _______ call later. (posibilidad)
4. He knows every line of the film. → He _______ have seen it many times. (deducción)
5. That _______ be Maria at the door — she said she'd come. (posibilidad)

**Soluciones:**
1. can't
2. must
3. might / may
4. must
5. might / may / could

## Bloque 4 — Ejercicios de Nivel C1: Modales Perfectos

Los **Modales Perfectos** (modal + have + participio) son fundamentales en niveles avanzados.

### Ejercicio 4: Transforma las frases usando el Modal Perfecto correcto

Modelo: *It's a pity he didn't study harder.* → *He should have studied harder.*

1. I'm sure she didn't hear the alarm. → She _______ the alarm.
2. It was a mistake for him to invest in that company. → He _______ in that company.
3. Perhaps they left early. → They _______ early.
4. I'm certain he lied to us. → He _______ to us.
5. It was possible for her to finish on time, but she didn't. → She _______ on time.

**Soluciones:**
1. can't have heard
2. shouldn't have invested
3. might / may / could have left
4. must have lied
5. could have finished

## Bloque 5 — Ejercicios de Estilo Formal: Modales para Cambridge

En exámenes Cambridge, los modales también aparecen en transformaciones de frase (Part 4 de Use of English).

### Ejercicio 5: Key Word Transformation

Reescribe cada frase usando la palabra clave dada sin cambiar el significado.

1. *It's possible that he forgot the meeting.* (MIGHT)
   → He _______ the meeting.

2. *I'm sure she didn't take your umbrella.* (HAVE)
   → She can't _______ your umbrella.

3. *It wasn't necessary for you to bring food.* (NEED)
   → You _______ food.

4. *I'm certain he has been working all night.* (MUST)
   → He _______ all night.

5. *It was a mistake for her to resign.* (SHOULD)
   → She _______ her job.

**Soluciones:**
1. might have forgotten
2. have taken
3. needn't have brought / didn't need to bring
4. must have been working
5. shouldn't have resigned

## Errores Más Comunes con Verbos Modales

Como profesor de Cambridge, estos son los errores que veo con más frecuencia:

| Error Común | Corrección |
|-------------|------------|
| ~~He musted leave early~~ | He had to leave early |
| ~~She can to swim~~ | She can swim |
| ~~I am must go~~ | I must go |
| ~~Could you to help me?~~ | Could you help me? |
| ~~He should have study more~~ | He should have studied more |

---

## Consejos para el Examen Cambridge

- **Must vs Have to**: En negative, tienen significados opuestos. *Must not* = prohibición; *Don't have to* = no es necesario.
- **Could have / Should have / Might have**: Siempre van seguidos del participio pasado del verbo principal.
- **May vs Might**: *Might* implica menor probabilidad que *may* en contextos formales.

Con práctica constante con estos ejercicios de **verbos modales en inglés**, estarás listo para cualquier examen o situación profesional que requiera expresar matices con precisión.
