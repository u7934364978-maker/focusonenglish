Cuando lees titulares como *"New regulations will be introduced next year"* o comunicados como *"The event will be held on Saturday"*, estás viendo la pasiva en futuro. Es una estructura muy común en anuncios oficiales, comunicados de empresa, noticias y cualquier tipo de texto formal que hable de lo que va a ocurrir.

## La estructura base: will be + participio

**Sujeto + will be + participio pasado**

Es la forma más directa de pasiva en futuro y la más frecuente:

- *The results will be announced on Friday.*
- *New policies will be implemented next year.*
- *All employees will be informed before the meeting.*
- *The bridge will be reopened in March.*
- *Your application will be reviewed within five working days.*

El participio no cambia nunca; lo que varía es el tiempo del auxiliar *will be*.

En negativa:
- *The details will not be revealed until the official press conference.*
- *The project won't be completed before the deadline.*

En pregunta:
- *Will the report be published online?*
- *When will the decision be made?*

## Going to be + participio: con planes previos

**Sujeto + am/is/are going to be + participio pasado**

Se usa cuando hay una intención o plan establecido de antemano, igual que en la activa:

- *The road is going to be closed for repairs next month.*
- *Three new hospitals are going to be built in the region.*
- *The annual report is going to be presented at the shareholders' meeting.*

En la práctica, especialmente en inglés formal y escrito, *will be* es más frecuente en pasiva. *Going to be* suena ligeramente más coloquial.

## Present Continuous como futuro: is being + participio

Cuando algo está programado para el futuro inmediato o hay un plan firme, a veces se usa el present continuous pasivo:

- *The meeting is being held tomorrow at 10am.* (ya está organizado)
- *The new members are being introduced at the gala tonight.*
- *A special screening is being organised for next week.*

Este uso transmite que hay preparativos ya en marcha. Es más informal que *will be*.

## Future Perfect Pasiva: will have been + participio

Para hablar de acciones que estarán terminadas antes de un punto futuro concreto:

**Sujeto + will have been + participio pasado**

- *By December, the project will have been completed.*
- *By the time you read this, the decision will have been made.*
- *All the data will have been processed before the conference.*

Es una estructura más avanzada y poco frecuente en conversación, pero habitual en contratos, planes de proyecto y textos académicos formales.

## Usos típicos de la pasiva en futuro

**En comunicados y anuncios oficiales:**
- *The new CEO will be appointed next month.*
- *All contracts will be reviewed in the upcoming audit.*
- *The policy will be revised before the end of the year.*

**En noticias y periodismo:**
- *A new law is expected to be passed before the summer recess.*
- *The suspect will be charged with three counts of fraud.*
- *The report will be released to the public next Tuesday.*

**En contextos académicos:**
- *The findings will be discussed at the annual symposium.*
- *Data will be collected over the following six months.*

**En atención al cliente y comercio:**
- *Your order will be shipped within 48 hours.*
- *A refund will be processed in 5-7 working days.*
- *You will be contacted by our team shortly.*

## La pasiva en futuro con modales

Cuando añades modales (must, should, may, might, can), la estructura es:

**modal + be + participio** (sin *will*, porque el modal ya expresa el futuro o la modalidad)

- *The form must be submitted by next Friday.*
- *The equipment should be tested before the launch.*
- *The event may be postponed due to weather conditions.*
- *This approach could be adopted in future projects.*

Si quieres combinar *will* con un modal de deducción (raro pero posible):
- *The report will probably be ready by Monday.* (aquí *will* es el futuro y *probably* el matiz)

## Comparativa rápida de opciones de futuro pasivo

| Estructura | Ejemplo | Matiz |
|---|---|---|
| will be + participio | *It will be announced.* | Predicción/decisión |
| going to be + participio | *It is going to be rebuilt.* | Plan establecido |
| is being + participio | *It is being organized.* | Evento futuro ya en marcha |
| will have been + participio | *It will have been completed by then.* | Futuro perfecto |
| modal + be + participio | *It should be finished soon.* | Posibilidad/obligación |

## Ejercicios

Transforma estas frases a pasiva en futuro:

1. They will announce the winner tomorrow.
→ *The winner will be announced tomorrow.*

2. The company is going to close three branches.
→ *Three branches are going to be closed.*

3. Someone will inform all the clients by email.
→ *All the clients will be informed by email.*

4. They will have processed all applications by May.
→ *All applications will have been processed by May.*

5. The committee will review your proposal.
→ *Your proposal will be reviewed by the committee.*

La pasiva en futuro es especialmente útil cuando escribes correos formales, informes o comunicados donde quieres centrar el foco en lo que ocurrirá, no en quién lo hará. Una vez que la incorporas a tu escritura, el nivel sube de forma visible.
