When you need to see a manager or a client, it is important to ask for an appointment politely. You can ask "Are you available at ten o'clock?" or "Can we meet tomorrow?". Using the word "available" is a professional way to ask if someone is free.

Once you and the other person agree on a time, you should confirm it clearly. Phrases like "That works for me" or "See you then" are perfect for finalizing the plans. Always double-check the time to avoid any misunderstandings.

Scheduling meetings is a key part of administrative work. By mastering these simple phrases, you can coordinate tasks and appointments with confidence and professionalism. Practice these questions with your team today!
